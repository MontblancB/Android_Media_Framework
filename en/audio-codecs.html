AOSP Audio Codecs

Comprehensive guide to audio codecs, formats, and specifications supported by Android

1 Audio Codec Overview

Android supports a wide variety of audio codecs, with required and recommended codecs defined in the CDD (Compatibility Definition Document).

CDD Requirements: Android devices must support the mandatory audio codecs defined in the CDD. This ensures app developers can rely on the availability of certain codecs.

2 AAC Codec Family

AAC (Advanced Audio Coding) is the most widely used audio codec on Android, supporting multiple profiles.

AAC-LC (Low Complexity)
CDD Required Decode Encode

The most basic AAC profile, offering high compatibility and good audio quality. Used by most streaming services and music files.

Specifications

Sample Rate: 8kHz ~ 96kHz
Channels: Mono, Stereo, 5.1
Bitrate: 8 ~ 320 kbps
Container: MP4, M4A, 3GP, MKV, ADTS
Recommended Sample Rates:
44100 Hz 48000 Hz 96000 Hz
HE-AAC v1 (AAC+)
CDD Required Decode

Uses SBR (Spectral Band Replication) to provide excellent quality at low bitrates. Ideal for streaming services and radio broadcasting.

Specifications

Sample Rate: 8kHz ~ 48kHz
Channels: Mono, Stereo
Bitrate: 24 ~ 128 kbps
Feature: SBR (Spectral Band Replication)
HE-AAC v2 (eAAC+)
CDD Required Decode

Uses SBR + PS (Parametric Stereo) to provide stereo quality even at very low bitrates (24~48kbps).

Specifications

Sample Rate: 8kHz ~ 48kHz
Channels: Stereo (with PS)
Bitrate: 24 ~ 64 kbps
Feature: SBR + PS
AAC ELD (Enhanced Low Delay)
CDD Required Decode Encode

Low-latency codec designed for real-time communication (VoIP, video conferencing). Provides algorithmic delay under 15ms.

Specifications

Sample Rate: 16kHz ~ 48kHz
Channels: Mono, Stereo
Latency: < 15ms
Use Case: VoIP, Real-time Communication
xHE-AAC (Extended HE-AAC)
Recommended Decode Android 9+

The latest AAC profile supporting a wide range from 12kbps ultra-low to high bitrates. Based on USAC (Unified Speech and Audio Coding).

Specifications

Sample Rate: 8kHz ~ 48kHz
Bitrate: 12 ~ 500 kbps
Feature: Unified Speech+Audio, Loudness Management

3 Lossless Codecs

Compresses original audio data without loss, providing CD quality or better.

FLAC (Free Lossless Audio Codec)
CDD Required Decode Encode

The most widely used open-source lossless codec. Offers 40~60% compression ratio with fast decoding speed.

Specifications

Sample Rate: 1Hz ~ 655,350Hz
Bit Depth: 4 ~ 32-bit
Channels: 1 ~ 8 channels
Container: .flac, MKV, OGG
Hi-Res Audio Sample Rates:
44100 Hz (CD) 48000 Hz 88200 Hz 96000 Hz 176400 Hz 192000 Hz
ALAC (Apple Lossless Audio Codec)
Optional Decode

Lossless codec developed by Apple. Primarily used by iTunes and Apple Music, also supported for decoding on Android.

Specifications

Sample Rate: 1Hz ~ 384kHz
Bit Depth: 16/24/32-bit
Channels: 1 ~ 8 channels
Container: M4A, MOV, CAF
PCM / WAV (Uncompressed)
CDD Required Decode Encode

Uncompressed raw audio data. Used for editing and mastering, but results in large file sizes.

Specifications

Sample Rate: 8kHz ~ 192kHz
Bit Depth: 8/16/24/32-bit (int), 32-bit (float)
Channels: Mono ~ 7.1
Container: .wav, .aiff

4 Voice/Speech Codecs

Low-bitrate codecs optimized for voice calls and voice recording.

AMR-NB (Narrowband)
CDD Required Decode Encode

Standard codec used for 2G/3G voice calls.

Specifications

Sample Rate: 8000 Hz
Bitrate: 4.75 ~ 12.2 kbps
Channels: Mono
Container: .amr, 3GP
AMR-WB (Wideband)
CDD Required Decode Encode (Optional)

High-quality voice codec used for HD Voice (VoLTE).

Specifications

Sample Rate: 16000 Hz
Bitrate: 6.6 ~ 23.85 kbps
Channels: Mono
Container: .awb, 3GP

5 Open Source Codecs

Royalty-free open source audio codecs.

Opus
CDD Required Decode Encode Android 5.0+

IETF standard codec that provides excellent performance for both voice and music. Used by WebRTC, Discord, WhatsApp, and more.

Specifications

Sample Rate: 8kHz ~ 48kHz
Bitrate: 6 ~ 510 kbps
Channels: 1 ~ 255 channels
Latency: 2.5ms ~ 60ms (configurable)
Opus Modes:
‚Ä¢ SILK: Voice optimized (low bitrate)
‚Ä¢ CELT: Music optimized (high quality)
‚Ä¢ Hybrid: Combined voice+music
Vorbis (OGG)
CDD Required Decode

Open source lossy codec developed by Xiph.Org. Widely used in game audio and streaming services.

Specifications

Sample Rate: 8kHz ~ 192kHz
Bitrate: 45 ~ 500 kbps
Channels: 1 ~ 255 channels
Container: .ogg, .ogv, MKV, WebM
MP3 (MPEG-1 Audio Layer 3)
CDD Required Decode

The most well-known audio format. Patents expired in 2017, now freely usable.

Specifications

Sample Rate: 8kHz ~ 48kHz
Bitrate: 8 ~ 320 kbps (CBR/VBR)
Channels: Mono, Stereo, Joint Stereo
Container: .mp3

6 Spatial Audio

Immersive audio codecs providing 3D audio experiences.

Dolby AC-4
Optional Decode Android 9+

Dolby's next-generation audio codec optimized for broadcast, streaming, and mobile. The default codec for Dolby Atmos content.

Specifications

Sample Rate: 48kHz
Bitrate: 64 ~ 2048 kbps
Channels: Up to 7.1.4 (Dolby Atmos)
Feature: Object-based Audio, Loudness Management
MPEG-H 3D Audio
Optional Decode Android 13+

MPEG standard next-generation 3D audio codec. Adopted for Korea's ATSC 3.0 broadcast standard.

Specifications

Sample Rate: 14.7kHz ~ 48kHz
Channels: Up to 64 channels + Objects
Feature: Personalized Audio, Loudness Management

7 Sample Rate & Bit Depth

Key parameters that determine audio quality.

Nyquist Theorem: Sample rate must be at least twice the maximum frequency to be reproduced. To reproduce human hearing range (20Hz~20kHz), a minimum of 40kHz is required.

9 Codec Selection Guide

Choosing the optimal audio codec based on use case.

10 API Reference

Key APIs for working with audio codecs on Android.

Use MediaCodecList to query the list of codecs supported by the device. 
// Query supported codecs
val codecList = MediaCodecList(MediaCodecList.ALL_CODECS)
for (codecInfo in codecList.codecInfos) {
    if (codecInfo.isEncoder) {
        Log.d("Codec", "Encoder: ${codecInfo.name}")
    } else {
        Log.d("Codec", "Decoder: ${codecInfo.name}")
    }

    for (type in codecInfo.supportedTypes) {
        Log.d("Codec", "  MIME: $type")
        val caps = codecInfo.getCapabilitiesForType(type)
        val audioCapabilities = caps.audioCapabilities

        audioCapabilities?.let {
            Log.d("Codec", "  Sample Rates: ${it.supportedSampleRates?.toList()}")
            Log.d("Codec", "  Bitrate Range: ${it.bitrateRange}")
            Log.d("Codec", "  Max Channels: ${it.maxInputChannelCount}")
        }
    }
}
